🔌 Plugged In… but Disconnected

The image shows a person with a screen for a head — fully absorbed into a device, while being literally plugged into the wall. It's a powerful metaphor for how modern technology has become part of us — not just something we use, but something we are constantly feeding and being drained by.

In today's world, technology is everywhere. We use it for learning, working, and staying connected. But at what cost? Many people, especially teenagers, are spending hours glued to screens — scrolling without purpose, gaming for entire nights, and constantly checking notifications. It feels normal… but it's not. We're losing real moments. Face-to-face conversations are replaced with emojis. Time with family is replaced with headphones. The mind never rests.

This is technology addiction. It doesn't come with warnings like cigarettes, but its damage is real — on our sleep, focus, emotions, and even self-esteem. The project Detoxify is created to raise awareness and help people take control. It's not about quitting technology, it's about finding balance. Sometimes, the healthiest thing we can do is to unplug — to reconnect with the world, with people, and most importantly, with ourselves.

The Hidden Impact of Our Digital Habit

When habits become harmful.....

Harms of PlayStation

Too much gaming can lead to poor academic performance and lack of focus at school or work. When gaming becomes a daily habit for long hours, it affects memory, lowers motivation, and reduces the desire to learn or achieve goals.

Harms of Smartphone

Spending hours on social media can cause isolation, low self-esteem, and even depression. Social comparison, cyberbullying, and constant notifications can damage mental health and increase stress levels, especially among teens.

Harms of screen

Overusing screens can ruin sleep quality and cause tiredness during the day. The blue light from devices interferes with melatonin production, leading to difficulty sleeping, poor mood, and reduced concentration.

Harms of Ignoring

Tech addiction can make us miss valuable time with family and real-world connections. It creates emotional distance, weakens relationships, and may lead to feelings of loneliness even when surrounded by loved ones.

Mental Health impact

Addiction to digital devices can increase stress, anxiety, and a sense of disconnection from real life. Constant engagement with screens leaves no time for relaxation or reflection, making it harder to cope with real-life problems.

Time Wasting on

Excessive gaming or browsing can steal time from important tasks like studying, hobbies, or exercise. Procrastination becomes a habit, and users often find themselves stuck in a cycle of short-term pleasure and long-term regret.

Some Features that Reflect Our Technology Addiction

When convenience turns into dependence…

Harm of PlayStation – Solutions

Set daily gaming limits, take frequent breaks, and balance screen time with physical and social activities. Avoid violent or addictive content and prioritize real-life interaction.

Harm of Smartphones – Solutions

Turn off non-essential notifications, use focus mode, and schedule screen-free hours. Replace unnecessary scrolling with hobbies or time with family and friends.

Harm of Screens – Solutions

Follow the 20-20-20 rule to protect your eyes, reduce screen brightness at night, and take breaks often. Avoid screens at least one hour before sleeping.

Harm of Ignoring Others – Solutions

Be present in conversations, avoid checking your phone when with others, and practice listening. Real-life connections need your attention more than your screen does.

Mental Health Impact – Solutions

Reduce social media use, talk about your feelings, and take care of your sleep and diet. Practice mindfulness and take regular digital detox moments for balance.

Time Wasting – Solutions

Set specific goals before going online, use productivity apps like Forest, and keep a daily schedule. Replace wasted time with meaningful activities like reading or learning.
